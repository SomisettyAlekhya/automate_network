SYN Flood attacks exploit the TCP three-way handshake by sending excessive SYN requests without completing the connection.
This causes half-open connections and resource exhaustion on the target server.

High SYN packet frequency from a single source IP may indicate a potential denial of service attempt.
Repeated incomplete TCP handshakes are a behavioral signature of SYN Flood attacks.

Port Scanning involves sending connection requests to multiple ports to identify active services.
A high number of unique destination ports contacted within a short time window may indicate reconnaissance behavior.

Attackers perform port scanning before exploitation to discover vulnerable services.
Port scanning is often the first phase in the cyber attack lifecycle.

MITRE ATT&CK T1498 represents Network Denial of Service under the Impact tactic.
This technique aims to disrupt availability of services by overwhelming network resources.

MITRE ATTACK T1046 represents Network Service Discovery under the Discovery tactic.
This technique is used to identify listening services and exposed applications on target systems.

Confidence scoring combines rule-based detection and anomaly detection to reduce false positives.
Severity levels are determined based on weighted risk evaluation of behavioral indicators.

Anomaly detection identifies deviations from normal traffic patterns without requiring labeled attack data.
Isolation Forest detects anomalies by isolating rare behavior in feature space.

Stateful tracking of IP behavior improves detection accuracy over single-packet analysis.
Security monitoring systems rely on aggregated behavioral metrics rather than individual events.

Structured JSON logging enables integration with SIEM platforms such as Splunk or ELK stack.
Automated response simulation mimics firewall or intrusion prevention system (IPS) behavior.

Denial of Service (DoS) attacks aim to make systems unavailable to legitimate users.
Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks use multiple compromised systems to amplify impact.

Reconnaissance activities are typically low-noise but high-value indicators of future attacks.
Early detection of reconnaissance reduces likelihood of successful exploitation.

Behavioral thresholds help differentiate normal traffic spikes from malicious activity.
Security systems must balance detection sensitivity and alert fatigue.
